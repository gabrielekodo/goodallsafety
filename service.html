Our services

Quality Control tests

Quality control (QC) represents the basic level of managing safety and quality in diagnostic radiology. In this spirit, we conduct Quality Control tests to check the performance of the equipment under routine clinical conditions.

A Radiation safety officer at Goodall Safety Conducting Quality Control tests on KV accuracy, HVL and Normalised dose on a Fixed X-ray machine at a hospital in Fort Portal town in Uganda

Radiation survey measurements

We conduct radiation protection surveys as a way of directly measuring radiation levels and identify potential leakage through breaks or voids in shielding. We perform these surveys on: Diagnostic fluoroscopic and radiographic equipment. This is done to enable our clients understand their standing on shielding against radiation leakage, the radiation leakage levels and same surveys are always submitted to the National regulator when requested for or during application for licensing.

A Radiation safety officer at Goodall Safety Conducting radiation survey measurements a Baggage X-rat scanner machine at a Hotel in Kampala, Uganda.

Monitoring of Radiation workers

We help facilities in ensuring that their workers are routinely monitored using dosimeters. A dosimeter measures the cumulative energy absorbed as a consequence of exposure to ionizing radiation. Personal dosimeters must be worn by radiation workers to measure their radiation exposure and this we support.

Documentation support for regulatory compliance.

At your disposal to support your facility in establishment of radiation safety and radiation security compliance documents. These range from Quality Control programs, Safety assessment reports, Qualified Expert reports, Application forms for licensing under the Atomic Energy Regulatory body of Uganda and specific trainings in radiation safety and security.
